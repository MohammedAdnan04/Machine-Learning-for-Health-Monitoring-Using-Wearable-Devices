## Overview

This repository contains the supporting materials for an abridged literature review on **machine learning and deep learning applied to wearable health monitoring devices**. The review examines 10 open-access research papers organised into 5 thematic categories, covering the current state, key challenges, and future directions of this rapidly growing field.

## Papers Reviewed

1. Sabry et al. (2022) — Machine Learning for Healthcare Wearable Devices: The Big Picture. *Journal of Healthcare Engineering.* https://doi.org/10.1155/2022/4653923

2. Shajari et al. (2023) — The Emergence of AI-Based Wearable Sensors for Digital Health Technology: A Review. *Sensors.* https://doi.org/10.3390/s23239498

3. Prieto-Avalos et al. (2022) — Wearable Devices for Physical Monitoring of Heart: A Review. *Biosensors.* https://doi.org/10.3390/bios12050292

4. Huda et al. (2020) — A Low-cost, Low-energy Wearable ECG System with Cloud-Based Arrhythmia Detection. *IEEE TENSYMP.* https://doi.org/10.1109/TENSYMP50017.2020.9230619

5. Gedam & Paul (2021) — A Review on Mental Stress Detection Using Wearable Sensors and Machine Learning Techniques. *IEEE Access.* https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2021.3085502

6. Radha et al. (2021) — A Deep Transfer Learning Approach for Wearable Sleep Stage Classification with Photoplethysmography. *npj Digital Medicine.* https://doi.org/10.1038/s41746-021-00510-8

7. Li et al. (2020) — Bi-LSTM Network for Multimodal Continuous Human Activity Recognition and Fall Detection. *IEEE Sensors Journal.* https://doi.org/10.1109/JSEN.2019.2946099

8. Mohammad et al. (2023) — An Enhanced Ensemble Deep Neural Network Approach for Elderly Fall Detection System Based on Wearable Sensors. *Sensors.* https://doi.org/10.3390/s23104774

9. Shaik et al. (2023) — Remote Patient Monitoring Using Artificial Intelligence: Current State, Applications, and Challenges. *WIREs Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery.* https://doi.org/10.1002/widm.1485

10. Islam et al. (2023) — Deep Learning-Based IoT System for Remote Monitoring and Early Detection of Health Issues in Real-Time. *Sensors.* https://doi.org/10.3390/s23115204
